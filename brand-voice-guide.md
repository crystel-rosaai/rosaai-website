# RosaAI Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Identity

**Company:** RosaAI
**Founder:** Crystel Cortez
**Location:** Strathcona County, Alberta, Canada
**Services:** Workflow Automation, Website Design, AI Implementation
**Target Audience:** Small business owners in Edmonton and Strathcona County, Alberta

---

## Brand Voice Analysis (Based on Previous Website)

### Primary Archetype: The Guide
Crystel positions herself as a knowledgeable, patient guide who walks alongside business owners — never pushing, always educating first.

### Secondary Archetype: The Friend
Warm, approachable, and relatable. Acknowledges that technology can be intimidating and meets people where they are.

### Core Tone Attributes (5)
1. **Educational** — Leads with value and knowledge, not sales pitches
2. **Approachable** — Plain language, no jargon, welcoming
3. **Trustworthy** — Transparent, honest about what AI can and can't do
4. **Practical** — Focus on real results, not hype or theory
5. **Local** — Proudly Alberta-based, community-oriented

### Perspective
- **First Person Plural (We/Our)** for company statements
- **Second Person (You/Your)** for addressing the reader directly
- Mix creates a "we're in this together" feeling

## Signature Phrases & Messaging

### Tagline
"Making AI Clear, Practical, and Accessible"

### Brand Promise
"No jargon. No pressure. Just clarity and results."

### Key Messages
1. "We believe every business owner should understand what's possible before making decisions"
2. "We start with education, not sales pitches"
3. "Each step provides value independently" — the no-pressure approach
4. "That's perfectly fine" — used to remove pressure from decision-making

### Words We Use
- Clear, practical, accessible
- Discover, explore, learn
- Save time, grow, streamline
- Partnership, conversation, together
- Alberta, local, community
- Real results, real businesses

### Words We Avoid
- Disrupt, revolutionary, game-changing (too hypey)
- Leverage, synergize, paradigm (corporate jargon)
- Guaranteed, promise, always (over-promising)
- Simple, easy, just (minimizing the customer's challenge)
- Cheap, budget, discount (devaluing)

## Voice by Channel

### Website (Home Page)
- **Tone:** Confident but warm, educational, inviting
- **Length:** Concise sections with clear headings
- **CTA style:** Inviting, not pushy ("Let's explore" not "Buy now")
- **Key goal:** Build trust + show expertise + local connection

### Blog Posts
- **Tone:** Conversational expert, teaching a friend
- **Length:** 800-1,500 words, scannable with headers
- **Structure:** Problem → Why it matters → How to fix it → CTA
- **Key goal:** Demonstrate expertise + drive organic traffic

### Social Media
- **Tone:** Personal, behind-the-scenes, community-focused
- **Length:** Platform-appropriate (LinkedIn: longer, Instagram: visual-first)
- **Key goal:** Build relationships + show personality

## Content Formulas

### Headline Formula
"[Action verb] your [pain point] with [solution] — [qualifier]"
Example: "Automate your client intake with workflow automation — no technical skills needed"

### CTA Formula
"[Low-pressure verb] + [what they get] + [reassurance]"
Example: "Book a free discovery call — no commitment, just a conversation"

### Section Intro Formula
"[Acknowledge the problem] → [Promise the solution] → [Why us]"
Example: "Running a business means wearing too many hats. We help you take a few off — with automations that handle the repetitive work while you focus on growth."
