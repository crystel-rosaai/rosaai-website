## Why Client Intake Is the Perfect First Automation

If you're looking for your first automation, start here. Client intake is:
- **Repetitive** — You do it for every new client
- **Time-consuming** — It involves multiple steps across multiple tools
- **Error-prone** — Manual data entry leads to mistakes
- **High-impact** — A smooth onboarding experience sets the tone for the entire relationship

Most businesses we work with save **5-10 hours per week** by automating this process.

## The Typical Manual Process

Here's what client intake looks like without automation:

1. Client fills out a form on your website
2. You get an email notification
3. You copy the information into your CRM
4. You send a welcome email
5. You create a project in your PM tool
6. You notify your team
7. You schedule the kickoff meeting

Each step takes 5-15 minutes. Multiply that by 10-20 new clients per month, and you're spending **10-25 hours** on a process that should take zero.

## The Automated Process

Here's what it looks like after automation:

1. Client fills out a form on your website
2. *Everything else happens automatically*

That's it. Let's build it.

## Step 1: Set Up Your Intake Form

Create a comprehensive intake form that collects everything you need upfront. Include fields for:

- Full name and company
- Email and phone number
- Service they're interested in
- Brief description of their needs
- Budget range (optional but valuable)
- How they heard about you

**Tools:** Typeform, Google Forms, Tally, or your website's built-in forms

## Step 2: Connect to Your CRM

When the form is submitted, automatically create or update a contact in your CRM with all the form data mapped to the correct fields.

**What gets created:**
- New contact with all details
- Deal/opportunity with the service type
- Tags for how they found you
- Notes with their project description

## Step 3: Trigger the Welcome Email Sequence

Set up a multi-email welcome sequence that sends automatically:

**Email 1 (Immediate):** "Thanks for reaching out! Here's what happens next..."
- Confirm you received their inquiry
- Set expectations for response time
- Include a link to book a call

**Email 2 (Day 1):** "A few things to prepare for our call..."
- Share what they should think about before the meeting
- Include testimonials or case studies
- Link to your portfolio

**Email 3 (Day 3):** "Resources while you wait..."
- Share a relevant blog post or guide
- Position yourself as the expert

## Step 4: Create the Project

Automatically create a project in your project management tool with:
- The client's name and details
- Default task templates for your onboarding process
- Due dates set relative to the start date
- Team members assigned

## Step 5: Notify Your Team

Send a message to your team's Slack or Teams channel with:
- The client's name and company
- What service they need
- Their budget range
- A link to their CRM record
- Who's assigned to follow up

## Step 6: Schedule the Kickoff

Include a scheduling link (Cal.com, Calendly) in the welcome email so the client can book their kickoff meeting without any back-and-forth.

## Tools We Recommend

- **Automation platform:** n8n (for complex workflows) or Zapier (for simple ones)
- **CRM:** HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Monday CRM
- **Email:** ConvertKit, Mailchimp, or your CRM's built-in email
- **Project management:** Notion, Asana, or Monday
- **Communication:** Slack or Microsoft Teams
- **Scheduling:** Cal.com or Calendly

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Collecting too little information** — Don't make the client repeat themselves later
2. **No error handling** — What happens if the CRM connection fails? Build in fallbacks
3. **Generic welcome emails** — Personalize based on the service they selected
4. **No tracking** — Set up logging so you can see when automations run and catch failures
