## Why Automation Matters for Small Business

If you're running a small business, time is your most valuable resource. Every hour spent on repetitive tasks is an hour you could be spending on growth, client relationships, or strategy.

The good news? Many of the tasks eating up your time can be automated — and you don't need to be a tech expert to make it happen.

## 1. Client Onboarding

**The problem:** New client comes in, you manually add them to your CRM, send a welcome email, create a project folder, notify your team...

**The solution:** A single form submission triggers everything automatically. The client's info flows into your CRM, a welcome email sequence starts, a project is created in your PM tool, and your team gets a Slack notification.

**Time saved:** 2-3 hours per new client

## 2. Invoice Processing

**The problem:** Manually entering invoice data, matching to purchase orders, routing for approval.

**The solution:** AI-powered OCR extracts data from invoices, auto-matches to POs, and routes to the right approver based on amount thresholds.

**Time saved:** 5+ hours per week

## 3. Lead Follow-Up

**The problem:** Leads come in from your website but don't get followed up quickly enough.

**The solution:** Automated email sequences triggered by form submissions, with smart delays and personalization. Qualified leads get auto-booked for discovery calls.

**Time saved:** 3-4 hours per week

## 4. Appointment Reminders

**The problem:** No-shows cost you time and money.

**The solution:** Automated SMS and email reminders at 24 hours and 1 hour before appointments. One-click rescheduling for clients.

**Time saved:** 80% fewer no-shows

## 5. Weekly Reporting

**The problem:** Spending hours pulling data from multiple platforms to create weekly reports.

**The solution:** Automated data aggregation from Google Analytics, your CRM, and social media. PDF reports generated and emailed to stakeholders every Monday morning.

**Time saved:** 4+ hours per week

## Getting Started

You don't need to automate everything at once. Start with the workflow that causes you the most pain, and build from there. Most automations can be set up in a few days and pay for themselves within the first month.
