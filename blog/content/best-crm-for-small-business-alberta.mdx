## Why Your Business Needs a CRM

If you're still tracking clients in spreadsheets, sticky notes, or your head — you're leaving money on the table. A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system is the backbone of every successful business.

But with hundreds of options available, which one is right for *your* business?

## What to Look for in a CRM

Before comparing tools, know what matters most:

1. **Ease of use** — Will your team actually use it?
2. **Automation capabilities** — Can it automate follow-ups and task creation?
3. **Integration support** — Does it connect to your other tools?
4. **Pricing** — Does it fit your budget as you grow?
5. **Canadian data compliance** — Where is your data stored?

## Our Top Picks

### 1. HubSpot CRM

**Best for:** Businesses that want a free starting point with room to grow

HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely generous. You get contact management, deal tracking, email integration, and basic automation — all for $0.

**Pros:**
- Powerful free tier
- Excellent automation in paid tiers
- Great integration ecosystem
- Built-in email marketing

**Cons:**
- Gets expensive as you scale
- Some features locked behind premium tiers
- Can be overwhelming with too many features

**Price:** Free — $1,600+/month for Enterprise

### 2. Pipedrive

**Best for:** Sales-focused teams that want simplicity

Pipedrive is the CRM that salespeople actually enjoy using. Its visual pipeline makes it easy to see where every deal stands.

**Pros:**
- Incredibly intuitive interface
- Visual sales pipeline
- Strong mobile app
- Affordable pricing

**Cons:**
- Limited marketing features
- Reporting could be deeper
- Fewer integrations than HubSpot

**Price:** $14-99/user/month

### 3. Monday CRM

**Best for:** Teams that need project management + CRM in one place

If you're already using Monday.com for project management, adding their CRM makes sense. Everything lives in one platform.

**Pros:**
- Flexible and customizable
- Great for project-based businesses
- Strong automation builder
- Visual and intuitive

**Cons:**
- CRM features are newer, less mature
- Can get complex with customization
- Per-seat pricing adds up

**Price:** $12-28/seat/month

## Which CRM Works Best with Automation?

Since we specialize in workflow automation, here's how each CRM integrates:

- **HubSpot** — Best native automation + excellent API for custom integrations
- **Pipedrive** — Clean API, great with n8n and Zapier
- **Monday** — Strong automation builder, good API support

All three work well with n8n, Zapier, and Make for custom automations.

## Our Recommendation

For most small businesses in Alberta:

1. **Start with HubSpot Free** — It's hard to beat free, and you can always upgrade
2. **Switch to Pipedrive** if you're primarily sales-focused and want a simpler experience
3. **Use Monday CRM** if your team already uses Monday for project management
