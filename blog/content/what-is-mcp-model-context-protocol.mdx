## AI Is Getting Smarter — But It Needs Context

You've probably used ChatGPT or Claude to write emails or brainstorm ideas. It's impressive, but you've also noticed something: the AI doesn't know anything about *your* business. It doesn't know your clients, your processes, or your data.

That's where the Model Context Protocol (MCP) comes in.

## MCP in Plain Language

Think of MCP as a universal adapter for AI. Just like USB-C lets you plug any device into any charger, MCP lets AI tools plug into any data source — your CRM, your calendar, your documents, your database.

Before MCP, connecting AI to your business data required custom coding for every single integration. Now, with MCP, it's standardized.

## Why This Matters for Your Business

### Before MCP
- AI tools worked in isolation
- Every integration was custom-built
- Connecting AI to your CRM required a developer
- Each AI tool needed its own connectors

### After MCP
- AI can access your business data securely
- One protocol works across all AI tools
- Faster, cheaper integrations
- Your AI assistant actually knows your business

## Real-World Examples

### AI That Knows Your Clients

Imagine an AI assistant that can:
- Pull up a client's history before a meeting
- Draft a proposal based on their specific needs and past conversations
- Automatically update your CRM after every interaction
- Answer "what's the status of the Smith project?" by checking your actual project management tool

With MCP, all of this is possible without custom development.

### Smarter Automation

MCP makes workflow automation more intelligent. Instead of simple "if this, then that" rules, your automations can:
- Use AI to make decisions based on real business context
- Understand the content of documents, not just their metadata
- Route tasks based on priority and complexity, not just simple rules

## Who's Using MCP?

MCP was created by Anthropic (the makers of Claude) and is being adopted by major technology companies. It's already supported by:
- Claude and other AI assistants
- Development tools like VS Code
- Automation platforms
- Business software integrations
